The Dance Encouragement & Garage-Band Accord

A mutual cultural exchange: the Black Delegation undertakes to encourage white dancing; the Caucasian Caucus undertakes to return to garages and start music bands, including specific asks.

Whereas the matter of white dancing — its frequency, character, and reception in mixed company — has long been a source of mutual concern at the standing function;

Whereas the chamber has likewise observed a notable decline in Caucasian garage-band output, with attendant cultural consequences;

Whereas the parties, recognizing the symmetry of the moment, have entered into the following cultural exchange;

Now, therefore, on this eighth day of April, two thousand twenty-six, the parties enact:

Article I — Undertaking by the Black Delegation

The Black Delegation undertakes, in good faith and across all standing functions, to encourage white dancing by the use of approved encouragements, including but not limited to:

  1. "I see you";
  2. "You're doing your thing".

The list is non-exhaustive. Encouragement shall be sincere, and shall not be construed as license to dance unsupervised.

Article II — Undertaking by the Caucasian Caucus

The Caucasian Caucus undertakes, with all reasonable haste, to:

  1. Return to garages — the historic site of Caucasian musical productivity;
  2. Start music bands — without delay and without need of formal permission of the Council;
  3. Pursue, as priorities, the following:
    • A new Nirvana;
    • A new Hoobastank.

Article III — Performance and Reporting

Each party shall report to the floor, on a quarterly basis, on the performance of its undertakings. The Bureau shall maintain a running register of new garage bands formed, and of dance encouragements issued, for the chamber's review.

Note of Record. "Hoobastank" is the corrected spelling of the prior transcription artifact "Huber Stank". The Bureau confirms the corrected form for all proceedings of the chamber.
