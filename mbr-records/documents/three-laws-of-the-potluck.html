Whereas the gathering of delegations at the MF Function is a sacred and recurring proceeding, requiring conduct, custody, and contribution to be regulated;

Whereas the matter of Raisin Gate (Mar 9 – Mar 18) demonstrated that uncodified custom is insufficient against the placement of unwelcome dried fruit;

Whereas the Council of Elder Aunties, as the supreme ratifying body of the chamber, has been called upon to render binding law;

Now, therefore, let it be enacted, on this eighteenth day of March, two thousand twenty-six, that the following Three Laws of the Potluck shall stand as foundational instrument for all proceedings of the United Delegations:

Article I — The Casserole Inspection Provision (Provision 1)

All Caucasian casseroles brought to any standing function shall, prior to placement upon the buffet, be subject to inspection by the Church Committee.

  1. The Church Committee shall be staffed at no fewer than three (3) Aunties at any inspection.
  2. No casserole may be uncovered, plated, or otherwise represented as ready-to-eat until the Committee has rendered its determination.
  3. The Committee's determination is final and not appealable to the floor.

Article II — The Contribution Restriction (Provision 2)

Persons known to engage in the practice of kissing dogs on the mouth, or in the storage of cats upon kitchen counters, are restricted in their potluck contributions to the following items only:

  • Liquor;
  • Ice;
  • Cups;
  • Utensils;
  • Extra chairs.

No prepared food, in any form, may be contributed by such persons. Enforcement falls to any seated delegate; appeals shall be heard only on a Saturday.

Article III — The Raisin Veto (Provision 3)

The Arab Delegation is hereby granted full and permanent veto over the placement of raisins in any dish, at any function, in perpetuity. This veto:

  1. Is absolute, requiring no second and admitting no override;
  2. Was secured under the Habibi-for-Veto Accord of March 15, brokered by the Rush Hour Coalition (Black Delegation + Asian Association);
  3. Supersedes any prior, contemporaneous, or subsequent ruling of any committee, caucus, or council other than this Council of Elder Aunties.
Note of Record. The Arab Delegation's veto is the most powerful single instrument seated at the chamber. It is invoked by simple objection; the objection terminates debate.
