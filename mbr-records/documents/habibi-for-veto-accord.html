The Habibi-for-Veto Accord

A brokered settlement of Raisin Gate by the Rush Hour Coalition (Black Delegation + Asian Association), conferring "Habibi" upon the Caucasian Caucus and a permanent raisin veto upon the Arab Delegation.

Whereas the matter of Raisin Gate, opened on March 9, 2026, threatened the good order of the chamber over a period of nine (9) days, and risked the deployment of the Cornflake Raisin Contingency Plan as escalation;

Whereas the Rush Hour Coalition — being the joint diplomatic instrument of the Black Delegation and the Asian Association — interposed its good offices on March 15 to propose a structural settlement;

Whereas the parties have, after due consideration, agreed to the following terms of mutual benefit;

Now, therefore, the following Habibi-for-Veto Accord is hereby enacted:

Article I — Conferral of "Habibi"

The Arab Delegation hereby grants to the Caucasian Caucus and, by extension, to all delegates of the chamber, the use of the address "Habibi", in good faith and without restriction beyond ordinary good manners.

The Coalition of Habibis is, by this accord, recognized as a standing cross-delegation body, with its ratified address at the House of Habibi.

Article II — Conferral of the Raisin Veto

In return, the Caucasian Caucus and all other delegations cede to the Arab Delegation a full and permanent veto over the placement of raisins in any dish at any function of the chamber. The veto is:

  1. Absolute — no second is required, no override is admitted;
  2. Perpetual — it does not lapse;
  3. Singular — it is held by the Arab Delegation alone, and is not transferable.

Article III — Codification

The veto granted under Article II shall be codified, on March 18, 2026, as Provision 3 of the Three Laws of the Potluck, by separate filing of the Council of Elder Aunties (see MBR-FND-001). Codification shall not diminish, alter, or amend the terms of this accord, but shall confirm them as foundational law.

Article IV — Subsequent Repentance

For the avoidance of doubt: the Caucasian Caucus's separate concession on March 17 of "one year of whimsical skedaddling", imposed as repentance by the Irish Association, is acknowledged but does not form part of this accord, and is filed separately as a decree (see MBR-DEC-2026-003).

Note of Record. The Arab Delegation's veto is, in operation, the most powerful single instrument seated at the chamber. It is invoked by simple objection; the objection terminates debate.
