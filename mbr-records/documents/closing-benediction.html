The Closing Benediction

The standing liturgical decree fixing the official closing benediction of all proceedings of the chamber.

Whereas the chamber, having no settled close, had on prior occasions ended its proceedings with varying and ad-hoc valedictions of inconsistent dignity;

Whereas the Council of Elder Aunties has determined that the closing should be a single, fixed, and unalterable benediction;

Now, therefore, the Council decrees:

The Benediction

God is good all the time,
and all the time, God is good.

The benediction shall be spoken — or sung, where the spirit moves — at the close of every proceeding of the chamber. It is the official sign-off of all MF Function proceedings.

Article I — Form

The form is fixed. The text shall not be paraphrased, abbreviated, or otherwise altered. The two halves are equal in standing; both must be uttered in the same close.

Article II — Standing

The benediction supersedes any prior practice. Any delegate who wishes to speak after the benediction must obtain leave of the chair, and shall be entered into the record only on a procedural basis.

Liturgical Note. The benediction is, in effect, the closing punctuation of every standing function. Where it has not been spoken, the proceeding is not deemed adjourned for the record.
