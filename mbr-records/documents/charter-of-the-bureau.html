Charter of the Multicultural Bureau of Records

Establishing the Bureau as the standing index of negotiations, accords, leases, gifts, and unresolved matters of the United Delegations.

Whereas the proceedings of the MF Function have, since the third day of March, two thousand twenty-six, generated a continuous and growing body of trades, accords, leases, gifts, rulings, and unresolved matters;

Whereas the chamber has long required a standing institution to receive, index, cross-reference, and certify the proceedings of the United Delegations;

Whereas the Armenian Delegation, Toronto Chapter, has come forward and offered to undertake this office in service of the chamber;

Now, therefore, let it be enacted, on this second day of May, two thousand twenty-six, that the Multicultural Bureau of Records is hereby established under the following Articles:

Article I — Establishment

There is hereby established, as a standing institution of the United Delegations, the Multicultural Bureau of Records (the "Bureau"). The Bureau shall be the official, public, and binding index of all proceedings of the chamber.

Article II — Mandate

The Bureau is charged with the receipt, indexing, and certified preservation of:

  1. All negotiations opened on the floor, whether concluded or pending;
  2. All delegations seated, recognized, or formed;
  3. All finalized trades, leases, concessions, and gifts;
  4. All ongoing discussions and matters under committee review;
  5. All unresolved issues, including those past deadline.

Each entry shall be cross-referenced to its originating communiqué, filed by the chair, the Hon. @georgebrettolson.

Article III — Designation of Record Keeper

The Armenian Delegation, Toronto Chapter is hereby confirmed as the official Record Keeper of the MFF. The Record Keeper shall:

  1. Maintain the standing index in good order and accurate to the latest filing;
  2. Certify documents, accords, and rulings under the seal of the Bureau;
  3. Issue corrections, amendments, and addenda upon receipt of new filings;
  4. Hold the Bureau in trust on behalf of the chamber, neutral as between delegations.
Note of Record. The Record Keeper, in the exercise of its duties, holds no voting privilege on matters substantive to the chamber and shall recuse itself from all questions on which it would otherwise be a party.

Article IV — Address and Continuity

The Bureau shall maintain a public address at mbofr.com, accessible to all delegates and to the public. The address shall be the canonical reference for all certified records, and shall be updated upon the receipt of each new filing.

Article V — Amendments

This Charter may be amended only by ratifying resolution of the MF Function, with concurrence of the Council of Elder Aunties. Procedural matters within the Bureau's mandate may be regulated by the Record Keeper without further ratification.
