The Nas Fade Ruling

The formal ruling of the Black Barber Caucus disposing of the matter of the haircut also called the "Travis Kelce Fade".

Whereas the haircut commonly displayed in popular media has, in recent months, been variously described as the "Travis Kelce Fade", suggesting an attribution it does not properly bear;

Whereas the Black Barber Caucus retains, by long usage and undisputed authority, jurisdiction over the naming, authentication, and historical attribution of all such cuts;

Whereas a forensic audit has been duly completed by the Caucus, examining clipper-line geometry, taper graduation, and historical antecedent;

Now, therefore, the Black Barber Caucus rules as follows:

Holding

The haircut, having been subjected to forensic audit, is hereby declared a historical clerical heir. It shall hereby and shall ever remain THE NAS FADE.

Reasoning

  1. The cut presents the geometry, taper, and crown-line characteristic of the Nas-attributed silhouette of the late 1990s;
  2. Subsequent wearers, however prominent, are properly understood as bearers of an heirloom and not as originators;
  3. Naming follows attribution; attribution follows record. The record is clear.

Disposition

  1. The designation "Travis Kelce Fade" shall not be used in the proceedings of the chamber;
  2. The designation "The Nas Fade" is the only proper form;
  3. Any wearer of the cut, regardless of public profile, is recognized as wearing The Nas Fade and not any other named cut.
Motion. Motion to declare the cut THE NAS FADE: passed. The ruling is final and not appealable to the floor.
