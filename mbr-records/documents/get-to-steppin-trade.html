Whereas the Black Delegation has, on April 6, 2026, indicated its agreement in principle to return the phrase "Get to Steppin'" to general circulation;

Whereas the Caucasian Caucus has placed two terms in escrow as consideration;

Whereas the Caribbean Brigade has produced documents of long use of "over" as their version of the same address;

Whereas the matter has been formally referred to Supreme Delegate Martin Lawrence for final ruling, and no such ruling has yet issued;

Now, therefore, the following terms are filed in DRAFT for the record. They shall not bind any party until and unless ratified by Supreme Delegate Martin Lawrence:

Article I — Proposed Restoration by the Black Delegation

The Black Delegation would return to general circulation the phrase "Get to Steppin'", in its standard and contracted forms.

Article II — Proposed Caucasian Consideration

In exchange, the Caucasian Caucus has tendered two phrases, presently held in escrow with the Bureau:

  1. "Take a Hike, Pal / Buddy / Bucko" — restored under terms parallel to "Skating on Thin Ice" (see MBR-ACC-2026-002);
  2. "You're Barking Up the Wrong Tree, Buddy" — added at the Black Delegation's request.

Article III — Caribbean Brigade Note

The Caribbean Brigade has filed a note of record indicating that "over" has long served as their version of "Get to Steppin'". The note is acknowledged but does not, by itself, alter the terms of this draft. Any final ruling shall account for the Brigade's existing use.

Article IV — Conditions Precedent to Ratification

This accord shall ripen into law only upon:

  1. Receipt of a final ruling from Supreme Delegate Martin Lawrence;
  2. Filing of that ruling with the Bureau under the seal of the Record Keeper;
  3. Conversion of this DRAFT to a ratified instrument by addendum, dated and signed.
Status Note. No party shall act in reliance upon this DRAFT. Use of "Get to Steppin'" or the proposed Caucasian phrases without ratification shall be entered into the record as procedural lapse.
