The Mayo + Ketchup Shawarma Restoration

The retroactive ruling of the Council of Elder Aunties overruling the outcome of the April 4 roast battle and restoring custody to its rightful holders.

Whereas on April 1, 2026, the Caucasian Caucus, by way of the chair, declared mayo + ketchup on shawarma a Caucasian practice;

Whereas on April 3 the Rainbow Syndicate (Lesbian Delegation) threatened a flannel and Subaru embargo and the revocation of "it's giving";

Whereas on April 3 the Ginger Gang Gang declared Code Red, and Josh Means challenged the chair to a roast battle for veto power;

Whereas the roast battle was held on April 4 and the chair lost;

Whereas the Council of Elder Aunties, exercising its supreme retroactive authority, has reviewed the entire matter and rendered the following ruling:

Holding

Mayo + ketchup on shawarma has, by the supreme authority of the Council of Elder Aunties, ALWAYS belonged to the Puerto Ricans. The custody is original; it is not a transfer; it is restored, not granted.

Article I — Disposition of Prior Claims

  1. The Caucasian Caucus's declaration of April 1 is VOID AB INITIO;
  2. The Rainbow Syndicate's threats of April 3 are not formally activated; "it's giving" status is contested but not revoked;
  3. The Ginger Gang Gang's Code Red is de-escalated;
  4. The roast battle outcome of April 4, in which Josh Means defeated the chair, is OVERRULED as a matter of jurisdiction; veto power may not be transferred via roast battle when supreme custody has not yet been established.

Article II — Restoration to the Puerto Ricans

Custody of mayo + ketchup on shawarma is hereby restored to the Puerto Rican Delegation, in perpetuity. The restoration is:

  1. Retroactive to the beginning of all proceedings of the chamber;
  2. Not subject to challenge by any subsequent roast battle;
  3. Inclusive of all condiment combinations on shawarma involving mayo, ketchup, or both.
Note of Record. The Council's retroactive authority is supreme. Where a matter has been improperly disposed of, the Council may correct the record without further process. The principle is settled.
