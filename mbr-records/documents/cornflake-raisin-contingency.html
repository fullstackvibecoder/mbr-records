The Cornflake Raisin Contingency Plan

The standing emergency-escalation procedure of the chamber, invoked when terms cannot be reached by deadline. First invoked March 14, 2026, on the naming vote.

Whereas the chamber, in its conduct of negotiations, periodically reaches an impasse where neither party will move and time is running short;

Whereas the Arab Delegation's permanent veto over raisins (see MBR-FND-001, Article III) is the most powerful single instrument of the chamber, but does not address all impasses;

Whereas the chamber requires a measured emergency-escalation procedure short of full collapse;

Now, therefore, the floor establishes the following standing decree:

Article I — Conditions of Invocation

The Cornflake Raisin Contingency Plan may be invoked when, and only when:

  1. A negotiation has reached a published deadline;
  2. No agreement has been ratified by that deadline;
  3. One or more delegations would otherwise lose their place on the floor through the impasse.

Article II — The Plan

Upon invocation, the Plan operates as follows: cornflakes, by emergency authority, may be presented at the next standing function with raisins already mixed in, in such a manner that delegates must address the impasse to remove them. Use is illustrative; the threat alone has, to date, sufficed in every case.

Article III — Limits

The Plan does not override:

  1. The Arab Delegation's permanent veto over raisin placement (which trumps the Plan);
  2. Any standing closure of the chamber on a Sunday;
  3. Any matter properly before the Council of Elder Aunties.
Note of Record. The Plan was first invoked on March 14, 2026, in connection with the Shindig / Hoedown / MFF naming vote (see MBR-ACC-2026-003). Invocation produced a vote within the deadline; the Plan was not deployed.
