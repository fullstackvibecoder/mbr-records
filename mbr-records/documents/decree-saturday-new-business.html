Decree on Saturday New Business

Standing decree designating Saturdays as the day of new-business submissions to the floor, with attendant prohibitions during voting.

Whereas the chamber requires a regular and predictable channel for the introduction of new business;

Whereas the integrity of voting requires that the floor be insulated from undue elevation of any one delegate's input;

Now, therefore, the Council decrees:

Article I — Saturdays as New-Business Day

Every Saturday, an open floor is convened for delegates to propose new business in the comments to the day's communiqué. Submissions are accepted from any seated delegation, subgroup, or recognized observer.

Article II — Prohibitions During Voting

For the duration of any vote at any standing function, the following are prohibited:

  1. Pinning of any comment by the chair or any subordinate;
  2. Hearting of any comment by the chair or any subordinate;
  3. Any other action that would elevate one delegate's submission above the others on the floor.
Procedural Note. The Council of Elder Aunties reaffirms the protocol on each invocation. Violations are entered into the record as procedural lapse and may, in serious cases, result in re-vote.
